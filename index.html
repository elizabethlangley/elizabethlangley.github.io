Biology

Hello! My name is Elizabeth Langley and I am a second year Biology Student at UCR. In my high school career, I have always decided to take classes not because I had to take them but because I was genuinely interested in them. When I found out that my school offered a Biotechnology course in affiliation with a local community college, I knew this was the course for me. I’m absolutely fascinated with lab science and figuring out how systems work peak my curiosity. One of my favorite units in this class was about food science. My face lit up when I extracted the DNA from strawberries for the first time or made cheese using enzymes. This completely changed the way that I look at everyday items.

Outside of the classroom, I decided to further my quest for knowledge and understanding by volunteering for the Stanford Blood Center. This valuable experience in a medical environment gave me a first hand experience of lab science and medicine working hand in hand. I was given the opportunity to observe how transfusion medicine is being used in the real world to help patients. Seeing science in action, and the value it holds in helping the community motivates me to pursue this field.

This class and my volunteer experience are two examples of the many reasons that I want to major in Biology. I love how science is constantly evolving and changing. What was true yesterday may not be true today due to new discoveries and constant innovation. I want to continue to explore subjects that I love, and be an independent critical thinker.
